Chinese New Year Decorations

Preparations for the New Year begin a couple of weeks before the big day. This is a fun time full of anticipation and joy as we get ready for the thrill and excitement of the celebrations. According to the traditions, this is the time to clean the house. Before the New Year comes, it is one of the Chinese New Year customs to clean the house from top to bottom. Offering Sacrifices to Ancestors

Traditionally, households prepared for New Year's Eve by bringing their family's genealogical records, ancestral portraits, and memorial tablets .etc. Offering sacrifices to the ancestors is one of the most important folk customs of Spring Festival. After burning three bundles of incense and bowing to the ancestors, prayers were offered for a fruitful harvest in the coming year.

Giving gifts

Chinese New Year is a time for giving; whether in the form of the popular, traditional red packet filled with money (红包 hóngbāo); or in the form of other, material goods. But beware, even the most generous offering can be tainted if you show bad etiquette when giving a gift. Here are some examples of gifts:
  • New and Crisp Money
  • Tea
  • Clothes
Watching Lion and Dragon Dances

On Chinese New Year, dragons and other unique animals are made into huge puppets. In Chinese Culture, the lion symbolizes strength, stability and superiority, while the dragon represents power, boldness and excellence.Dragon and lion dances are intended to bring luck and prosperity, a sentiment that's very important to Chinese people.
