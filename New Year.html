Why is New Year celebrated?

What is New Year?
New Year is the time or day at which a new calendar year begins and the calendar's year count increments by one. Many cultures celebr ate the event in some manner. In the Gregorian calendar, the most widely used calendar system today, New Year occurs on January 1 (New Year's Day). This was also the first day of the year in the original Julian calendar and the Roman calendar (after 153 BC). Many people celebrate New Year's Eve to bid farewell to the year that ends and to welcome the New Year.
What is the tradition on New Year's Eve?
In many countries, New Year’s celebrations begin on the evening of December 31—New Year’s Eve—and continue into the early hours of January 1. Revelers often enjoy meals and snacks thought to bestow good luck for the coming year. In Spain and several other Spanish-speaking countries, people bolt down a dozen grapes-symbolizing their hopes for the months ahead-right before midnight.
